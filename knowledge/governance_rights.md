# COMPREHENSIVE WORKER RIGHTS (INTERNATIONAL & MIDDLE EAST)

## 1. PASSPORT & DOCUMENTS
- **PASSPORT POSSESSION:** In most countries (including Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar), it is ILLEGAL for an employer or sponsor (Kafeel) to confiscate your passport. You have the right to keep your original passport at all times.
- **IQAMA / ID CARD:** Your employer is responsible for providing and paying for your residency permit (Iqama). If they don't provide it, you are at risk of arrest, but it's the employer's legal obligation.

## 2. SALARY & PAYMENTS
- **ON-TIME PAYMENT:** Salary MUST be paid into your bank account (WPS - Wage Protection System) every month. 
- **NO DEDUCTIONS:** Employers cannot deduct money for "Visa Costs", "Work Permit", or "Insurance". These are the employer's costs by law.
- **OVERTIME:** Any work beyond 8 hours a day (or 48 hours a week) MUST be paid at overtime rates (usually 1.5x).

## 3. CONTRACTS
- **LANGUAGE:** You should have a copy of your contract in a language you understand (Bangla or English).
- **JOB DESCRIPTION:** You cannot be forced to do work that is not in your contract (e.g., if you are hired as an Electrician, they cannot force you to be a domestic worker).

## 4. HEALTH & SAFETY
- **PPE:** Employers must provide safety shoes, helmets, and gloves for free if the work is dangerous.
- **MEDICAL:** Employers must provide health insurance and pay for work-related injuries.
- **REST:** You are entitled to at least 1 day off per week and mandatory rest periods during heat (Mid-day work bans in Gulf countries).

## 5. EXIT & RETURN
- **EXIT PERMIT:** In many countries (like Qatar), you no longer need employer permission to leave the country. Check the current local law.
- **EMERGENCY LEAVE:** If a close family member dies, you have the legal right to take unpaid or paid leave to return home.

## 6. LEGAL AID & COMPLAINTS
- **BMET COMPLAINTS:** If you have been cheated by an agency in Bangladesh, you can file a formal complaint at the BMET office in Kakrail, Dhaka.
- **PROBASHI KALLYAN DESK:** Found at Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport (HSIA). Always visit them before departure if you have any last-minute doubts.
- **EMBASSY SUPPORT:** The Bangladesh Embassy in your host country has a "Labor Wing". Save their local hotline number immediately upon arrival.
- **WAGE EARNERS' WELFARE BOARD (WEWB):** Provides financial assistance to families if a worker dies or is seriously injured abroad.

## 7. FINANCIAL LITERACY
- **OFFICIAL CHANNELS:** Always send money through banks or exchange houses. Using "Hundi" is illegal and risky.
- **REMITTANCE INCENTIVE:** The Bangladesh government provides a cash incentive (e.g., 2.5%) for money sent through legal channels.
- **LOANS:** Probashi Kallyan Bank provides low-interest loans for migration. Avoid high-interest loans from local moneylenders.

# FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)
1. **Can I change my job?** In UAE and Qatar, yes, usually after completing your contract or with specific notice. In Saudi, it's possible after 1 year through the Qiwa platform.
2. **What if my Iqama is expired?** It is your employer's duty to renew it. If they don't, you can report them to the Labor Office (Maktab al-Amal).
3. **What is 'Free Visa'?** THERE IS NO SUCH THING AS A FREE VISA. This is a common scam. Every work visa must be tied to a specific employer or company by law.
4. **Is medical insurance mandatory?** Yes, in all Gulf countries, the employer must provide a health insurance card that covers basic treatments and emergency care.
